03 Common questions

Things people ask most.

How long is a typical install?+
From your first call to power-on is usually 30–45 days. Most of that is permit and PG&E paperwork — the actual on-roof install is a single day, occasionally two if it's a big system or a complicated roof.
Do you sell shared broker leads to other installers?+
No. You're calling me directly. The number you see on the site is my number. Nobody else gets your info.
What does a quote actually cost?+
Nothing. I come out, walk the roof, and send a one-page quote within 48 hours. If we don't end up working together, that's fine — no charge, no follow-up army.
What if my roof needs work first?+
I'll tell you straight up. If your roof is more than 15 years old and worn, solar on top of it doesn't make sense — you don't want to pull the system off in 5 years to re-roof. I can recommend roofers I trust, and we sequence the work right.
Are you NEM 2.0 or NEM 3.0?+
If you didn't have a system before April 2023, you're on NEM 3.0 (the new net-metering rules). I'll show you the math both ways — with and without a battery — so you can see why batteries matter more now.
What about the federal tax credit and SGIP?+
30% federal tax credit applies to most systems (and to batteries) through 2032. SGIP — California's storage rebate — varies by utility and equity tier. I'll spell out exactly what you'll get and when on the quote.
